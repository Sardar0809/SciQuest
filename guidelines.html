Community Guidelines

Rules and standards that keep SciQuest safe and fair for everyone

Last updated: July 2025. By using SciQuest, you agree to these guidelines. Violations may result in warnings, suspension, or permanent bans.

✅ What You SHOULD Do

  • Be honest about your qualifications, university, and field of study.
  • Write clear, detailed task descriptions so helpers know exactly what is needed.
  • Communicate respectfully with task partners via messages or WhatsApp.
  • Pay the agreed amount promptly after approving completed work.
  • Leave honest, constructive reviews based on actual experience.
  • Report suspicious or inappropriate content to the admin team.
  • Deliver quality work on time as a helper — your reputation depends on it.
  • Negotiate any changes to scope or timeline before work begins.

❌ What You MUST NOT Do

  • Post fake tasks or create fake accounts with fabricated credentials.
  • Refuse payment after receiving and using completed work.
  • Harass, threaten, or disrespect other students in any way.
  • Post personal or contact information of other users publicly.
  • Use SciQuest for commercial work unrelated to your own academic needs.
  • Leave malicious fake reviews to damage a student's reputation.
  • Misrepresent completed work as your own if submitting for grades.
  • Share your account login with others.

⚖️ Academic Integrity

SciQuest supports legitimate academic assistance. The following are always acceptable: survey participation, presentation design, data entry/analysis guidance, proofreading, tutoring, and research assistance.

It is your personal responsibility to ensure that any work you submit to your institution complies with their academic integrity policy. SciQuest is a support platform, not an academic dishonesty service.
